Vegetarian Italian Lasagna



Photo by alleksana from Pexels

This recipe is a delicious basic Italian lasagna. It's cheesey, garlicy, and hits all those lasagna notes.
You won't even notice there's no meat. I hope you enjoy this recipe as much as my family does.

Ingredients

  • 9 Lasagna Noodles
  • 2 TBSP Olive Oil
  • 1 Shallot
  • 1 Bulb Garlic
  • 2 16oz Cans of Beans (I use Cannelli)
  • 1 28oz Can Crushed Tomatoes
  • 1 28oz Can Diced Tomatoes
  • 1 6oz Can Tomatoe Paste
  • ¼ TSP Crushed Rerd Pepper Flakes
  • 1 TVSP Sugar
  • 1 ½ TSP Basil, Dried
  • 1 TSP Italian Season
  • Saly and Pepper (amounts in directions)
  • 1 Egg
  • 12 oz Ricotta Cheese
  • ½ Cup Shredded Parmesan Cheese
  • 2 Cups Shredded Mozzarella Cheese (or an Italian Blend)
  • ½ Cup Fresh Chopped Parsley

Directions

  1. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package directions.
    Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 350°F and grease 13x9 inch a baking dish.
    As the noodles cook, you can work on the other components.
    When cooked, drain the noodles. If you're not yet ready to combine the lasagna,
    place noodles in a pot or bowl of cold water to prevent noodles from sticking together.

  2. In a large skillet over medium heat, saute the shallot and garlic in olive oil until tender.
    Add both cans of tomatoes, tomato paste, crushed red pepper flakes, sugar, basil, Italian
    seasoning, 1 tsp salt and ½ tsp pepper. Simmer on low for 1 1/2 hours.
    After removing from heat, stir in 1/4 cup of the fresh chopped parsley.

  3. In a medium bowl combine the beaten egg, ricotta, Parmesan, and mozzarella (or Italian Blend)
    cheeses, 1/2 tsp salt and the other 1/4 cup of fresh chopped parsley

  4. Spread approximately 1/2 - 1 cup of bean/sauce mixture on the bottom of the pan, layer with 3 noodles.
    Top noodles with 1/2 the cheese mixture. Repeat by topping that with 1/3 of remaining bean/sauce
    mixture, 3 noodles, and the last 1/2 of cheese mixture. Finally, add another 1/3 of the
    bean/sauce mixture, layer 3 noodles, the last 1/3 of the bean mixture, and top with a sprinkling
    of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.

  5. Cover with foil and bake for 45 minutes. Uncover and bake for an additional 15 minutes or until the
    cheese on top becomes bubbly. Let stand at least 10 minutes before cutting and servinges.
